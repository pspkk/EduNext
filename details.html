Brutalist Resurgence in Urban Centers: A Monographic Review

person Dr. Aris Thorne
calendar_today October 1998
book Journal of Modern Structures

Abstract

This paper examines the subtle re-emergence of brutalist architectural philosophies in contemporary urban planning. Moving away from the heavily criticized concrete monoliths of the mid-20th century, modern architects are adopting the raw material honesty and monumental forms of brutalism while integrating sustainable practices and lighter spatial configurations. The analysis covers case studies across three major metropolitan areas, highlighting how structural weight is used to anchor transient cityscapes.
